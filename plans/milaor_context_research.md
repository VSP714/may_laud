## Basic Information
- **Municipality**: Milaor, Camarines Sur
- **Province**: Camarines Sur, Bicol Region
- **Region**: Region V (Bicol Region)
- **Population**: Approximately 33,000 (2020 census)
- **Classification**: 4th class municipality
- **Land Area**: 33.64 km²
- **Barangays**: 20 barangays
- **Zip Code**: 4413

## Geographical Context
- Located in the central part of Camarines Sur
- Bounded by Naga City to the north, Gainza to the east, Camaligan to the west
- Near the Bicol River (one of the largest rivers in the region)
- Agricultural land with rice fields, coconut plantations

## Cultural and Historical Significance
- **Festivals**: 
  - "Pabasa ng Pasyon" during Holy Week (traditional chanting of Christ's passion)
  - Town Fiesta in honor of St. John the Baptist (June 24)
- **Local Products**: Rice, corn, coconut, pili nuts, abaca
- **Language**: Majority speak Central Bikol (Bikol Naga), with Tagalog and English understood
- **Religion**: Predominantly Roman Catholic

## Flood Risk Areas
Milaor is prone to flooding due to:
- Proximity to Bicol River
- Low-lying areas, especially barangays near the river
- Heavy rainfall during typhoon season (July-November)

## Economic Activities
- **Agriculture**: Rice farming, coconut cultivation, vegetable gardening
- **Fishing**: Along Bicol River and nearby water bodies
- **Small-scale Enterprises**: Sari-sari stores, local eateries, handicrafts
- **Government Employment**: Municipal offices, public schools

## Transportation
- **Tricycles**: Primary mode of local transportation
- **Jeepneys**: Connect to Naga City and neighboring towns
- **Buses**: For longer distances to Manila or Legazpi

## Education
- **Public Elementary Schools**: 7 schools
- **Public High Schools**: 2 schools
- **Private Schools**: Several private institutions
- **Tertiary Education**: Residents often go to Naga City for college

- **Barangay Health Stations** - In various barangays
- **Private Clinics** - Limited number
- **Hospitals**: Nearest major hospital is in Naga City

## Design Implications for Milaud App

### Color Scheme
Based on Milaor's municipal seal and Philippine flag:
- **Primary Blue**: `#0056A3` (representing peace, truth, justice)
- **Philippine Gold**: `#FDB913` (from Philippine flag, representing wealth)
- **Philippine Red**: `#CE1126` (from Philippine flag, representing courage)
- **White**: `#FFFFFF` (purity)
- **Green**: `#00A859` (agriculture, nature)

### Typography
- **Headings**: Use bold, clear fonts for readability
- **Body**: Simple, clean fonts for information-heavy screens
- **Consider Bicol translations** for key terms

### Imagery
- Use local photographs of Milaor scenery
- Icons representing local products (rice, coconut, fish)
- Municipal seal for official communications

### Content Localization
- Key screens should have Bicol translations available
- Use local terminology (e.g., "barangay" instead of "village")
- Reference local landmarks for orientation

### Feature Priorities Based on Local Needs
1. **Flood Alert System** - Critical due to flood-prone location
2. **Document Request** - For barangay clearance, certificates
3. **Agricultural Advisories** - Weather, planting schedules
4. **Local News & Announcements** - Municipal updates
5. **Emergency Contacts** - Localized to Milaor services
